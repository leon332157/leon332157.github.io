Prosecution Summary:

At this week's press conference covering the trial of George Milton the State of California (Prosecution) is accusing George Milton of the murder of Lennie Small, charges of 1st degree murder, meaning the murder was planned in advance. The prosecution lawyers claim George Milton, a migrant worker, murdered his traveling partner Lennie Small using a Luger shotgun belonging formerly to Carlson Smith a fellow ranch worker of both the men. The accused murder happened in a brush about 4 miles from the ranch. There were no witnesses to the actual murder. There was an unknown motive that caused George Milton to kill Lennie Small, which he planned in advance. Prosecution also states the men from the ranch found Lennie, dead at the crime scene.The men were on a hunt to kill Lennie Small after Small murdered Curley Tyler’s wife. Prosecution plans to bring witnesses Curley Tyler(The boss’s son), John Tyler(the boss), and Carlson Smith(a roommate of Milton and Small) to the stand.

Defense Summary:

The defense lawyers have stated that Milton had killed Lennie to spare him from Curley, who had his wife killed by Lennie. Contradictory to what the prosecutors say, the defense states that Milton had not planned the murder of Lennie Small beforehand, since he had no motive before Curley started seeking revenge. Not only that, but George and Lennie were extremely close companions and it is unlikely that George wanted to kill Lennie. Finally the defense stated that they will bring Slim Johnson, a worker at the ranch, Candy Rogers, another worker at the ranch, [Crooks], a black man who works at the ranch and George Milton himself to the witness stand.
