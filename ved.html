Jury Verdict

By Gaeun

The jury met today at 9:04 AM to discuss the verdict of George Milton, who was accused of first degree murder of Lennie Small. After four days of listening to witnesses and lawyers
in the court, the jury members have quite a lot to say about the trial.
The first vote started off with 17 to 1 for majority not guilty, conducted by the jury foreman, Hope Stafford. She started off by
stating that Mr. Milton’s intentions were to kill Lennie Small in the most painless way possible, asking the members of the jury, “Why would have George killed Lennie if he didn’t care about him?” Henry Min, arguing for guilty although he voted oppositely, countered her reasoning. He claimed that despite Mr. Milton’s intentions, it was still a premeditated murder that Mr. Milton described. The second vote was determined the same as before - 17 to 1, favoring not guilty. Ensi Kamberi, adding to what Henry Min argued before, brought up the incident when Mr. Milton ordered Lennie Small to jump into the river just for a laugh. She stated that George treated Lennie in a dominant, puppet-like way. Reid Siggers, arguing for not guilty, counter-argued that George Milton needed to have that kind of power over him because Lennie Small has a intellectual disability, and that Mr. Milton was significantly more cautious after seeing Mr. Small in distress. The opposing argument fired back, claiming that Mr. Milton should’ve known the full risk when he made Lennie Small drown himself in that situation. At this moment, our media team was asked to leave the deliberation room to receive the final verdict in the courtroom. The jury foreman announced, after talking through heated arguments going back and forth, the defendant as not guilty.
