Migration of The Okies

By Zach
Losing all their farmland “Okies”, migrant workers from Oklahoma moved to the west coast in search of agricultural work. They would follow relatives to the coast of Washington, California, and Oregon. The move with children is much harder. Florence Thompson, whose family moved west during the Great Depression following the crop cycle in California. All she could say was "We just existed! We survived, let's put it that way." California saw seven thousand new migrant workers like Florence and her family in a month. The migration got so intense that 136 deputies from Los Angeles were sent to turn back immigrants without any money. Bordering states like Arizona were rattled that all the migrant workers with no money would get dumped into their state. Even with the successful cotton crop there was not enough work for the “Okies”. The books of John Steinbeck closely relate to the lives of these migrant workers and the life he describes is slightly dramatized versions of their life. They lived in tents or in the back of trucks. The “Okies” caught the sympathy of the rest of America because they were white but the Mexican and Filipino workers who supplied the seasonal crop work before the “Okies” were ignored.
