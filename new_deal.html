A Newer New Deal?

By: Sam

This January, President Roosevelt addressed the Congress in Washington to outing his plans for the Second New Deal. Since the stock market crash in 1929, the United States has been in an economic crisis with banks shutting down and unemployment at an alltime high. Four years after the stock market crash, President Roosevelt introduced the New Deal. The New Deal has not been a success because many of the jobs it has created are only temporary. Now, two years after the New Deal, President Roosevelt has a newer New Deal called the Second New Deal. The Second New Deal would attempt to tackle unemployment by offering millions of jobs to millions of people through the W.P.A. (Works Progress Administration), young people would also get job opportunities from the National Youth Administration and the Wagner Act would allow workers to form trade unions to negotiate pay. Additionally, the Second New Deal would provide security to the elderly with a new system called social security that would provide financial retired people and the Second New Deal would also provide electricity to rural areas. One could wonder if the Second New Deal will be helpful after Roosevelt’s last failure at getting the U.S. economy back on its feet. Since this new attempt does not involve trickle down economics, it is fair to say that this attempt will be more successful than the last. We can only wait and see the outcome of this newer New Deal.
